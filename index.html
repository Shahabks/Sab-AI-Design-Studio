Publications

  • Automatic Prompt Selection for Large Language Models
    PAKDD 2025 | From Prompt-Focus to RAG-Pipeline optimization

    Designing effective prompts either manually or automatically is challenging and time-consuming. Automatic prompt optimization won't offer flexibility or efficiency. Rather, the building blocks of a RAG-Pipeline should work in harmony to create an effective Knowledge Retrieval system. In this project, we study some potential effective approaches; focusing first on prompts, to automatically select the optimal prompt for a given input from a finite set of synthetic candidate prompts. Our approach consists of three steps: (1) clustering the training data and generating candidate prompts for each cluster using an LLM-based prompt generator; (2) synthesizing a dataset of input prompt-output tuples for training a prompt evaluator to rank the prompts based on their relevance to the input; (3) using the prompt evaluator to select the best prompt for a new input at test time. Our approach balances prompt generality and specificity and eliminates the need for resource-intensive training and inference.

  • When Giant Language Brains Just Aren't Enough! Domain Pizzazz with Knowledge Sparkle Dust
    Inductrial Technology 2023 | Infusing Domain-Knowledge into Large Language Models (LLM) Importance,; Challenges

    Adapting large language models (LLMs) for real-world business scenarios presents challenges. Our empirical study and analysis of this project have demonstrated promising outcomes. In this project, we employed an LLM that was enriched with additional knowledge from insurance policy rulebooks and external sources of knowledge like DBpedia. The enriched LLM exhibited improved understanding of human's structured and domain-specific concepts and enhanced reasoning abilities. The incorporation of domain-specific knowledge significantly enhances the accuracy of GPT-3.5 in insurance question answering tasks. These findings underscore the importance of integrating domain-specific knowledge and external resources to effectively address the complexity of business scenarios.

  • Make The Most of Prior Data: A Solution for Interactive Text Summarization with Preference Feedback
    NAACL 2022 | Text Summarization considering the subjectivity and human's preference

    Subjectivity reflects a person's thoughts and is pivotal when removing unwanted and redundant information in document summarization. This leads to general disagreement over what constitutes a good summary. Our research addresses this subjectivity by proposing a solution for interactive text summarization with preference feedback, empowering machines to better align with human preferences.
